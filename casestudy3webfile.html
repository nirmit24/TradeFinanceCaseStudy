The Challenge of Trust in Trade

International trade involves inherent risks for both importers and exporters. The fundamental conflict: the exporter wants payment before shipping, while the importer wants to receive goods before paying.

Payment Risk

The exporter fears the importer might default on payment after the goods have been shipped, leading to significant financial loss.

Shipment Risk

The importer worries the exporter might ship incorrect, substandard, or damaged goods, or fail to ship them at all after payment.

Logistical & Country Risk

Complexities like customs, political instability, or currency controls can disrupt the transaction and payment flow unexpectedly.

Documentary Fraud

Either party could be a victim of fraudulent documents, leading to payment for non-existent goods or loss of goods without payment.

The Solution: A Letter of Credit (LC)

A Letter of Credit is a legal and financial instrument from a bank that guarantees the exporter will be paid on behalf of the importer, provided the terms specified in the LC are met. It replaces the buyer's and seller's risk with the creditworthiness of banks.

Why LCs are Vital for Nigerian Pharma

Nigeria's pharmaceutical market heavily relies on imports to meet domestic demand for essential medicines. This reliance makes secure and reliable trade finance mechanisms like Letters of Credit indispensable for public health.

A Win-Win for Both Parties

The Letter of Credit creates a secure environment where both the importer and exporter can trade with confidence.

For the Nigerian Importer 🇳🇬

  • ✓Payment Assurance: Payment is only made when the exporter fulfills all terms of the LC, ensuring the correct goods are shipped on time.
  • ✓Proof of Creditworthiness: An issued LC demonstrates the importer's financial stability to the supplier.
  • ✓Access to Trade: Enables trade with new or risk-averse suppliers who would otherwise demand advance payment.

For the European Exporter 🇪🇺

  • ✓Mitigates Payment Risk: The bank's credit risk replaces the importer's, guaranteeing payment upon presenting compliant documents.
  • ✓Secure Market Entry: Allows for safe expansion into new or perceived high-risk markets like Nigeria.
  • ✓Financing Opportunities: The exporter can often use the LC to secure pre-shipment financing from their own bank.
